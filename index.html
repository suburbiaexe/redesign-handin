responsive redesign

the aim of this project is to practice the workflow of redesigning a simple website with improved design and accessibility choices.

usability problems

my chosen site

for this project, I chose to redesign the Hawes Fine Foods site, as it had a clean, simple aesthetic that I appreciated (but also room for improvement).

problems

  • usability
    • vertical scroll but no way to jump between sections.
    • main functionality is to view store inventory but there's no emphasis on the inventory section
  • memorability
    • technically fine, but a bit boring to look at
  • general
    • a lot of awkward negative space
    • disjointed headers between 'about' and 'gift card' sections

accessibility

the site actually scored a lot of accessibility points with WAVE, particularly with its strong contrast (which is to be expected with a black and white color scheme). however, a couple things were marked down:

  • most images were missing alt-tags
  • some header sizes were skipped, which WAVE noted could be confusing
    • (I don't know that I really agree with this, but it wasn't like I couldn't easily fix that)

one thing WAVE didn't mark that I noticed was font size. a lot of the site's text is quite small and I didn't like that I had to squint/zoom in on the site to read its content.

lo-fi prototype

process

I made lo-fi prototypes for 3 separate displays: desktop, tablet, and mobile. I used Balsamiq to create the general layout ideas I had to address some of the issues brought up earlier.

redesign

the site

the final site i created ended up being a little different from my initial prototypes, but the main ideas are still there. the font sizes are a lot larger, the spacing looks better (in my opinion) with the high-contrast alternating backgrounds, all the images have alt-tags, and the minimalistic essence of the site is still present.
